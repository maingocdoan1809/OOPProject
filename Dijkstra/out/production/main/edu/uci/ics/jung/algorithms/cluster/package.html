Mechanisms for identifying clusters in graphs. Where these clusters define disjoint sets of nodes, they may be used to define a NodePartition for more convenient manipulation of the node/set relationships. Current clustering algorithms include:
  • BicomponentClusterer: finds all subsets of nodes for which at least 2 nodes must be removed in order to disconnect the induced subgraphs.
  • EdgeBetweennessClusterer: identifies node clusters by removing the edges of the highest 'betweenness' scores (see the importance/scoring package).
  • VoltageClusterer: Clusters nodes based on their ranks as calculated by VoltageRanker.
  • WeakComponentNodeClusterer: Clusters nodes based on their membership in weakly connected components of a graph.
